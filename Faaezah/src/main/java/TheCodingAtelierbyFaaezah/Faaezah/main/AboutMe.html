Inspired by Her Grandparents

Faaezah’s summers in India were more than family visits — they were lessons in quiet leadership and compassion. Her grandfather, a retired professor, modeled the power of shared knowledge. Her grandmother, with gentle wisdom, showed how education could be an act of love. These moments shaped Faaezah’s belief that learning should be accessible to all. The Coding Atelier is her tribute to their legacy — a way to extend opportunity to children who’ve been left out of the digital world.

Why Digital Literacy Matters

Faaezah has seen firsthand how technology can widen the gap between privilege and possibility. For many children, especially in underserved communities, computers remain out of reach — not because they lack talent, but because they lack access. Through refurbished laptops, custom CodeKits, and hands-on workshops, she’s working to change that. Her goal is simple: make digital literacy feel possible, joyful, and empowering.

What makes her CodeKits different

The CodeKits Faaezah designs are more than learning tools — they’re invitations to explore. Built for children with little or no exposure to technology, each kit is layered with offline and online activities, unplugged challenges, and beginner-friendly software. Whether a child is six or sixteen, the kits are crafted to spark curiosity and build confidence before they ever touch a computer.

Where she Works

The Coding Atelier lives in two places close to Faaezah’s heart: India, where her family roots run deep, and Austin, Texas, where she’s grown up. From orphanages and universities to local libraries and elementary schools, she’s building partnerships that meet children where they are — with tools that help them grow.
